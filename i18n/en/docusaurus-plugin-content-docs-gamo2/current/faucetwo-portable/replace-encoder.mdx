# How to Replace the Encoder

<Steps>
<Step title="Disassemble the Knob">

Use a 2.0mm hex wrench (or screwdriver) to disassemble the knob. Note that each knob has two screws that need to be loosened.

![Step 01](/img/docs/gamo2/faucetwo-portable/0100.png)

</Step>
<Step title="Remove the Transparent Gasket">

Note that there is 1 transparent gasket on the encoder — be careful to remove it.

![Step 02](/img/docs/gamo2/faucetwo-portable/0800.png)

</Step>
<Step title="Remove the 3 Screws on the Back">

Remove the 3 screws on the bottom and take off the cover plate.

<div style={{display: 'flex', gap: '1rem', flexWrap: 'wrap'}}>

![Step 03a](/img/docs/gamo2/faucetwo-portable/0300.png)

![Step 03b](/img/docs/gamo2/faucetwo-portable/0400.png)

</div>

</Step>
<Step title="Loosen the Nut and Remove the Metal Washer">

Use pliers to loosen the nut and remove the metal washer.

![Step 04](/img/docs/gamo2/faucetwo-portable/0500.png)

</Step>
<Step title="Replace the Encoder">

Remove the 3PIN cable and connect it to the new encoder.

![Step 05](/img/docs/gamo2/faucetwo-portable/0600.png)

</Step>
<Step title="Reinstall the Encoder">

When reinstalling the encoder, be sure to place the wiring under the circuit board, and align the limit point on the encoder with the opening on the controller.

<div style={{display: 'flex', gap: '1rem', flexWrap: 'wrap'}}>

![Step 06a](/img/docs/gamo2/faucetwo-portable/0700.png)

![Step 06b](/img/docs/gamo2/faucetwo-portable/0701.png)

</div>

</Step>
<Step title="Reinstall the Metal Washer and Nut">

Use the metal washer and nut to reinstall the encoder, placing the transparent gasket at the top.

![Step 07](/img/docs/gamo2/faucetwo-portable/0800.png)

</Step>
<Step title="Align the Knob Screw">

There is a top screw inside the knob — align it with the flat (D-shaped) side of the encoder shaft for ease of installation.

![Step 08](/img/docs/gamo2/faucetwo-portable/0900.png)

</Step>
</Steps>

:::danger Important

When tightening the knob screw, if you feel it being pushed, just tighten it 1/4 turn. Otherwise, the plastic shaft of the encoder will be damaged.

:::
